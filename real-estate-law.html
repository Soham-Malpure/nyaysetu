Understanding Real Estate Law

Definition and Scope

Real Estate Law governs the ownership, use, and transfer of real property including land and buildings. It encompasses property transactions, landlord-tenant relations, property development, zoning regulations, title disputes, and real estate financing. This area of law ensures clear property rights and facilitates secure real estate transactions.

Key Areas: Property purchase/sale, lease agreements, title verification, property disputes, construction laws, environmental compliance, taxation, and regulatory approvals.

Common Transaction Issues

Potential Problems:
  • Title Defects: Unclear or disputed ownership, pending litigation
  • Encumbrances: Existing mortgages, liens, or legal claims
  • Approval Issues: Missing or invalid building approvals
  • Measurement Discrepancies: Actual area differs from documents
  • Pending Dues: Outstanding property taxes, utility bills
  • Zoning Violations: Property use not matching zoning requirements

Landmark Real Estate Cases

Indian Landmark Cases

M.S. Narayana Menon v. State of Kerala (2006)

Supreme Court ruling on property rights, establishing that rights in property cannot be taken away except by authority of law.

Sarla Verma v. Delhi Development Authority (2007)

Important case on compensation for land acquisition, establishing fair compensation principles.

Karnataka Board of Wakf v. Government of India (2004)

Supreme Court case clarifying ownership rights and religious property management.

Dagdu v. Rahimbi (2002)

Established principles of adverse possession and requirements for claiming title through possession.

Hemaji Waghaji Jat v. Bhikhabhai Khengarbhai Harijan (2009)

Supreme Court ruling on agricultural land ceiling laws and their constitutional validity.

DLF Universal Ltd. v. Directorate of Town and Country Planning (2008)

Important case on builder obligations and regulatory compliance in real estate development.

Property Rights Jurisprudence

RERA Compliance

Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016

RERA aims to regulate the real estate sector and protect the interests of homebuyers by ensuring transparency, accountability, and efficiency in real estate transactions.

Key Objectives:
  • Protect interests of homebuyers
  • Promote transparency in real estate sector
  • Ensure timely completion of projects
  • Create efficient dispute resolution mechanism
  • Establish real estate regulatory authority in each state

RERA Compliance Requirements

Project Registration

  • Mandatory for projects above 500 sq.m
  • Detailed project information disclosure
  • Quarterly progress reports
  • Financial details and approvals

Agent Registration

  • Real estate agents must register
  • Qualification and experience requirements
  • Annual renewal process
  • Code of conduct compliance

Buyer Protections

  • Right to accurate project information
  • Penalty for project delays
  • 5-year structural defect warranty
  • Standardized sale agreements

Financial Regulations

  • 70% of funds in escrow account
  • Funds used only for project
  • Regular financial reporting
  • Audit and compliance checks
